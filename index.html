Sloths: What are they?

They are tree-dwellers which spend their lives in the tropical rain forests located in Central and South America Their diet consists of leaves, twigs and buds and Sloths have a low metabolic rate, spending around 15 to 20 hours per day sleeping whilst hugging trees. A fun fact about this species is how they are excellent swimmers despite the fact that they have slow mobility! There are two different types of sloths : two-toed and three-toed and six species. Out of these six species of Sloths, the pygmy sloth is critically endangered and the maned sloth is vulnerable!

WorldWildLife.org, 2024

Crazy facts about sloths!

  • Sloths are so slow that algae can grow on their bodies, which actually helps with avoiding predators and surviving in the wild.
  • Sloths are seen to resemble monkeys due to their long arms and shaggy fur, but they are actually related to armadillos and anteaters!
  • Sloths are extremely picky when it comes to finding a place to poop once per week. They tend to defecate at the end of the tree of which they were enjoying the canopy.
